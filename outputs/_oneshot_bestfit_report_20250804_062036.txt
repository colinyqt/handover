=== Clause: 1.20 METERS AND INSTRUMENTS ===

Requirements (Original):
- Potential circuits suitably fused
- Accuracy Class 0.5
- Removable or hinged covers with dust exclusion gaskets
- Protective instruments with provision for locking
- Voltmeters with selector switches for phase-to-phase and phase-to-neutral voltage indication
- Ammeters with selector switches for indication of all phase currents
- Scale of length 90 degrees with external zero adjustment
- Meters and instruments fully tropicalised
- KWh meter of direct reading type
- Vibration absorbing material for meters/instruments in vibration-prone areas
- Integrating meters comply with BS 5685
- Flush pattern meters with square escutcheon plates finished matt black
- Indicating instruments comply with IEC 60051, IEC 61010, IEC 60529
- All terminals completely insulated

- Accuracy class 0.5

=== Clause: RMS Disturbances ===

Requirements (Original):
- The initiation of a trigger will cause the instrument to store the actual waveforms (voltage and current) during the disturbance. The waveform recording will include user-defined pre-event and post-event cycles.
- The initiation of a trigger will cause the instrument to store a trend plot of the rms voltage during the disturbance (both L-N and L-L rms plots will be recorded). This trend will include a minimum of 5 cycles of pre-trigger data, and 25 cycles of post-event data. The pre-event and post-event durations will be user-defined.
- The monitor shall characterize rms disturbances according to the requirements of IEC 61000-4-30.
- Both waveforms and rms plots will be recorded for rms disturbances.
- The unit will trigger on one-cycle or longer variations in the rms voltage level, outside user-defined thresholds. Triggering thresholds will be user-defined for both voltages (L-N and L-L) and currents.

Requirements (Condensed):
- The initiation of a trigger will cause the instrument to store the actual waveforms (voltage and current) during the disturbance. The waveform recording will include user-defined pre-event and post-event cycles.
- The initiation of a trigger will cause the instrument to store a trend plot of the rms voltage during the disturbance (both L-N and L-L rms plots will be recorded). This trend will include a minimum of 5 cycles of pre-trigger data, and 25 cycles of post-event data. The pre-event and post-event durations will be user-defined.
- The monitor shall characterize rms disturbances according to the requirements of IEC 61000-4-30.
- Both waveforms and rms plots will be recorded for rms disturbances.
- The unit will trigger on one-cycle or longer variations in the rms voltage level, outside user-defined thresholds. Triggering thresholds will be user-defined for both voltages (L-N and L-L) and currents.
